# Prediction Chains: From Portfolio Signals to Lifestyle Predictions

## The Core Method

Every prediction follows the same chain:

```
Portfolio Signal → Behavioral Pattern → Archetype Match → Domain-Specific Prediction
```

Multiple chains converging = high confidence.
Single chain = moderate confidence.
No chain from portfolio = low confidence, ask for context.

**CRITICAL: The predictions below are METHODS, not answers. The same method will produce DIFFERENT answers for different archetypes.**

---

## Phone Prediction

### Step 1: Determine Price Tier
- Estimate income from portfolio size + age
- Then look at the GAP between income and spending signals in the portfolio
- Big portfolio + value stocks = likely underspends → mid-tier phone
- Small portfolio + aspirational stocks = likely spends at/above income → premium phone
- Large portfolio + quality holdings = spends on quality but not status → premium-mainstream

### Step 2: Determine Brand Philosophy
- Quality-maximizer archetype → tends toward whatever they see as "best" (could be Apple, could be Samsung, could be Pixel depending on their tech orientation)
- Status-signaler archetype → whatever is most recognized (currently iPhone in India)
- Value archetype → whatever gives most specs-per-rupee (budget Android)
- Tech-first archetype → ecosystem-driven choice (all-Apple or all-Google)

### Step 3: Determine Hold Period
- Long-term stock holder → long phone holder (3-5+ years, current model is old)
- Frequent trader → frequent upgrader (1-2 years, has current or recent model)
- Moderate holder → moderate upgrader (2-3 years)

### Step 4: Determine Variant
- Top-variant bias: if someone consistently buys the best within their tier in other domains, they'll buy the top variant phone (Pro Max, Ultra, etc.)
- Base-model buyer: if portfolio shows "good enough" mentality (index funds only, no stock picking), they'll buy base model

## Car Prediction

### Step 1: Category
- SUV vs. Sedan vs. Hatchback is driven by:
  - Life stage (family → SUV/MPV likely)
  - Peer influence (if trend-follower archetype → SUV because that's what everyone buys)
  - Contrarian streak (if holds out-of-favor stocks → might drive sedan in SUV market)
  - Practical needs (city driving → hatchback/sedan, highway → SUV)

### Step 2: Brand Origin Preference
- Look for patterns in the portfolio:
  - Heavy Indian holdings + PSU preference → may prefer Indian brands (Tata, Mahindra, Maruti)
  - Japanese-quality signals (reliable blue chips, held forever) → may prefer Japanese (Honda, Toyota)
  - Aspirational/MNC holdings → may prefer Korean (Hyundai, Kia) or European
  - Tech-forward holdings → may prefer EV brands
- **Don't assume — this is a hypothesis to test**

### Step 3: Price Point
- Use the phone tier as a calibration
- If phone is premium-mainstream, car is likely ₹10-25L range
- If phone is luxury, car is likely ₹30L+ range
- If phone is budget, car is likely ₹5-12L range
- Cars are usually 1-2 tiers below what people could "afford" based on income — everyone underspends on cars relative to capacity

### Step 4: Age of Vehicle
- Stock holding period ≈ car holding period (remarkably consistent)
- Multi-year stock holder → car is 5-8+ years old
- Frequent churner → car is 1-3 years old

## Watch Prediction

### Step 1: Does This Person Wear a Watch?
- If portfolio is ultra-utilitarian (all ETFs, no individual personality in picks) → might not wear a watch
- If portfolio shows any aesthetic/quality signal → likely wears a watch
- Younger tech-forward archetype → might wear smartwatch instead of traditional

### Step 2: Price Tier
- Watch price tends to be 0.03-0.1% of portfolio value
- ₹10L portfolio → ₹3-10K watch (Casio, Titan, Fossil)
- ₹50L portfolio → ₹15-50K watch (Seiko, Tissot, Hamilton)
- ₹1Cr+ portfolio → ₹50K-2L watch (Grand Seiko, Longines, Omega) OR could be ₹30K if they're a value archetype
- Status-signaler with large portfolio → ₹2L+ (luxury Swiss)

### Step 3: Philosophy
- Quality-maximizer → craft-focused brands (values movement quality, finishing)
- Status-signaler → recognizable brands (the watch should be identifiable by others)
- Value-maximizer → functional brands (accurate, durable, no premium for name)
- Tech-first → smartwatch (Apple Watch, Garmin)

## TV Prediction

### Step 1: Brand
- Follow the same country-of-origin pattern detected in car/phone
- If Japanese affinity detected → Sony
- If Korean affinity → Samsung or LG
- If value-optimizing → Xiaomi, TCL, or budget models of major brands
- If doesn't care about brand → whatever was on sale or recommended

### Step 2: Size
- Quality-maximizers tend to buy larger than average (they research and find bigger = better value per inch)
- Value buyers buy the biggest they can afford
- Status buyers buy whatever's premium regardless of size
- Use living space estimate from portfolio (city, property signals)

## Hotel Prediction

### Step 1: Experience vs. Brand
- Quality-maximizer → hotel with best in-room experience, regardless of brand fame
- Status-signaler → most recognized hotel brand in the segment
- Value buyer → best rate, loyalty points optimizer
- Conservative → heritage brand, tradition, family-endorsed

### Step 2: The Stock-Hotel Paradox
- People who hold hotel stocks often DON'T stay at that chain
- Investment thesis ≠ personal preference
- Don't predict "holds Taj stock → stays at Taj"

## Banking Prediction

### Step 1: Recognize Two Separate Decisions
- **Daily bank** = driven by LOYALTY/CONVENIENCE (which bank they started with, employer tie-up, family bank)
- **Loan provider** = driven by OPTIMIZATION/EXECUTION (best rate, best process, best terms)
- These are USUALLY DIFFERENT banks
- Don't assume the same bank for both

### Step 2: Daily Bank Signals
- First-job bank often becomes permanent daily bank for loyalty-driven people
- Tech-savvy people may have switched to better digital banking UX
- Conservative archetypes → PSU banks (SBI, PNB, BOB)
- Tech-first archetypes → digital-first banks (Kotak 811, Jupiter, Fi)

### Step 3: Loan Bank Signals
- Optimization-driven → whoever has lowest rate
- Quality-driven → whoever has smoothest process
- Loyalty-driven → same as daily bank (doesn't separate the decisions)

## Travel Prediction

### Step 1: Destination Philosophy
- Value-conscious → destinations where their currency stretches (SEA for Indians)
- Status-signaler → aspirational destinations (Europe, US, Dubai)
- Experience-seeker → culturally rich destinations (Japan, Peru, Morocco)
- Comfort-seeker → familiar cultural contexts (for Indians: SEA, Middle East, Sri Lanka)
- Conservative → domestic, pilgrimages, family heritage sites

### Step 2: Cultural Affinity (REQUIRES ORIGIN + CURRENT CITY)
- This is the domain with lowest accuracy from portfolio alone
- Ask origin city/state before attempting travel predictions
- Travel for joy is emotional, not economic — financial data alone is insufficient

### Step 3: Travel Style
- Hold-forever archetype → researches heavily, plans in advance, repeat-visits places that worked
- Momentum archetype → spontaneous, tries new places, influenced by social media
- Value archetype → budget-optimized, group tours, deals-driven
- Premium archetype → planned, premium-mainstream hotels, curated experiences

## Clothing Prediction

### Step 1: Shopping Frequency
- Long holders → buys infrequently, repeat-purchases same items
- Churners → buys frequently, follows seasonal trends
- This maps directly from portfolio churn rate

### Step 2: Brand Philosophy
- Quality-maximizer → fabric/material quality over fashion (functional premium brands)
- Status-signaler → recognizable brands, visible logos
- Value buyer → local brands, sale-driven, quantity over quality
- Trend-follower → fast fashion brands
- Wellness-oriented → comfort-first, how it feels on the body

## Cultural & Emotional Predictions (Low Confidence)

### The Honest Disclaimer
Portfolio data CANNOT reliably predict:
- Favorite sports person
- Entertainment preferences (Bollywood, music, content)
- Comfort food
- Travel destinations chosen for emotional reasons
- Regional cultural identity

**For these, you NEED origin city + current city at minimum.** Even then, accuracy is ~40-50%.

**Always caveat these predictions** with: "This is where the portfolio-based model has lower accuracy. Your cultural identity is shaped by where you grew up and where you live, not just how you invest."

### Using Origin + City for Cultural Predictions
- Regional origin creates strong priors for food, entertainment, and emotional preferences
- Current city influences adopted habits (new food, new activities, peer group effects)
- The INTERSECTION of origin and current city is the most useful signal
- A person living in their home state vs. a migrant in a new city will have very different cultural profiles

## The Meta-Rules

### Consistency Check
After 3-4 predictions, check:
- Is the price tier consistent across domains? (±1 tier is normal, ±3 means archetype is wrong)
- Is the brand philosophy consistent? (same origin preference, same quality-vs-status tradeoff)
- Is the hold period consistent? (replaces phone and car and watch at similar frequency)

If inconsistent → the person has MULTIPLE OPERATING MODES. Identify which mode governs which domain.

### The Top Variant Rule
Once you identify someone's brand tier, ALWAYS predict the top variant within that tier. People who research extensively (which most investors do) converge on the best option within their budget. Predict the 65" not the 55". Predict the Pro Max not the Pro.

### The Two-Question Unlock
If you can ask just TWO questions before cultural/emotional predictions:
1. "Where are you originally from?" (origin city/state)
2. "What's your current city?"

These improve cultural prediction accuracy from ~30% to ~50-60%.

### Handling Misses
1. Acknowledge without over-apologizing
2. Ask: "What's the actual answer? What made you choose that?"
3. Classify the miss (environmental override / cultural signal / hidden dimension / wrong archetype)
4. Note which additional data source (food.md, content.md, location.md) would have prevented it
5. Update subsequent predictions based on the new information
