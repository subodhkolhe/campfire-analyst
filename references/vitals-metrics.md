# Campfire Vitals — Metric Definitions

## The Metaphor

The Campfire Vitals framework maps 8 biological metrics to assess physical readiness. Campfire Vitals maps 8 portfolio metrics to assess financial readiness. Each is scored 0-100.

---

## Metric 1: HRV → Diversification Quality (0-100)

**Biological:** Heart Rate Variability measures the body's ability to adapt to stress. High HRV = resilient, flexible system.

**Financial:** How well-distributed is the portfolio across uncorrelated assets? Can it absorb shocks without cascading failure?

**Scoring:**
- 90-100: Broad sector coverage, multi-asset (equity + gold + debt + international), no single sector > 25%, geographic diversification present
- 70-89: Good sector spread but missing 1-2 asset classes (e.g., no international or no debt), mild concentration
- 50-69: Moderate concentration (1-2 sectors > 30%), limited asset classes, domestic-only
- 30-49: Heavy concentration in few sectors, single asset class dominance, no hedging
- 0-29: Extreme concentration (1-2 stocks > 50% of portfolio), single sector, no diversification

**What to evaluate:**
- Count distinct sectors with meaningful allocation (>3%)
- Check for asset class mix (equity, gold, debt, international, cash)
- Measure Herfindahl concentration of top 5 positions
- Check for geographic diversification (international % of total)

---

## Metric 2: RHR → Cost Efficiency (0-100)

**Biological:** Resting Heart Rate — lower is better. Efficient heart does more with less effort.

**Financial:** How efficiently is the portfolio structured? Low expense ratios, minimal fragmentation, tax-optimized.

**Scoring:**
- 90-100: Consolidated holdings (same asset in 1-2 vehicles), direct equity + low-cost index funds, tax-efficient structure (SGBs, ELSS used well), minimal cash drag
- 70-89: Mostly efficient but some fragmentation (3-4 vehicles for same asset), reasonable expense ratios
- 50-69: Noticeable fragmentation (5+ vehicles for same asset class), mixed active/passive without clear strategy, some cash drag
- 30-49: Significant fragmentation, high expense ratio funds, poor tax planning, excess cash sitting idle
- 0-29: Extreme fragmentation, no consolidation strategy, regular-plan MFs instead of direct, large idle cash balances

**What to evaluate:**
- Count vehicles per asset class (Nifty trackers, gold instruments, etc.)
- Check if MFs are direct plans
- Measure cash/liquid as % of total (>10% = drag unless intentional)
- Look for regular vs. direct plan MFs

---

## Metric 3: Sleep Quality → Risk Calibration (0-100)

**Biological:** Sleep quality measures recovery depth. Poor sleep = accumulated stress.

**Financial:** Is the portfolio's risk level appropriate for the investor's apparent stage, style, and goals? Too much risk = sleepless nights. Too little = missed growth.

**Scoring:**
- 90-100: Risk level matches investor behavior (long holder has appropriate equity %, aggressive trader has appropriate hedges), gold/defensive allocation present and sized right (5-15%), no overleveraged positions
- 70-89: Mostly appropriate but minor mismatches (e.g., defensive allocation slightly too high or too low for the investor's revealed risk tolerance)
- 50-69: Some mismatch between portfolio risk and revealed behavior (e.g., holds aggressive small caps but also has 20%+ in gold/liquid suggesting anxiety)
- 30-49: Clear mismatch — portfolio is either much more aggressive or much more defensive than the investor's revealed style suggests they're comfortable with
- 0-29: Dangerous mismatch — leveraged F&O positions with no hedging, or 50%+ in cash/liquid for someone with a 10+ year horizon

**What to evaluate:**
- Compare equity allocation to apparent holding period and risk tolerance
- Check if defensive allocation (gold, debt, liquid) is proportionate
- Look for F&O usage vs. hedging presence
- Assess if small cap % is appropriate for portfolio size and investor temperament

---

## Metric 4: Strain → Growth Momentum (0-100)

**Biological:** Strain measures training intensity. Right strain = growth. Too much = injury. Too little = stagnation.

**Financial:** Is the portfolio generating healthy growth? Are positions compounding? Is new capital being deployed effectively?

**Scoring:**
- 90-100: Multiple multibaggers (5+), overall returns significantly above benchmark, both old (long-held) and new positions contributing, active SIP/accumulation visible
- 70-89: Solid returns with some standout positions, beating benchmark on most timeframes, evidence of continued investment
- 50-69: Returns roughly matching benchmark, few standout winners, portfolio growing but not outperforming
- 30-49: Returns below benchmark, limited winners, portfolio may be stagnating
- 0-29: Negative overall returns, multiple deep losers, no evidence of continued investment or rebalancing

**What to evaluate:**
- Count multibaggers (100%+ returns)
- Compare overall return to Nifty 50 over similar period
- Assess if winners are being held (not sold early)
- Check for evidence of continued accumulation (newer positions alongside old)

---

## Metric 5: Recovery → Rebalancing Discipline (0-100)

**Biological:** Recovery score measures readiness for the next challenge. Good recovery = sustainable performance.

**Financial:** Does the investor maintain their portfolio? Do they rebalance, consolidate, clean up dead positions, or just accumulate?

**Scoring:**
- 90-100: Clean portfolio with intentional sizing, no micro-positions (<₹5K with no thesis), periodic rebalancing visible, consolidated vehicles
- 70-89: Mostly maintained but some stale positions, mild fragmentation, occasional rebalancing
- 50-69: Accumulates without maintaining — adds new positions but rarely cleans old ones, growing fragmentation
- 30-49: Significant number of micro-positions, dead holdings, no visible rebalancing, asset class sprawl
- 0-29: Portfolio is a graveyard of past ideas — dozens of tiny positions, multiple vehicles for same asset, no maintenance whatsoever

**What to evaluate:**
- Count positions under ₹10K (micro-positions)
- Count vehicles per asset class (fragmentation)
- Look for deep losers held without thesis (>40% down, tiny position)
- Assess if sizing is intentional (conviction = larger) or random

---

## Metric 6: Respiratory Rate → Income Stream Health (0-100)

**Biological:** Respiratory rate indicates baseline metabolic health. Steady and efficient is optimal.

**Financial:** How healthy and diversified are the portfolio's income streams? Dividends, interest, capital gains potential.

**Scoring:**
- 90-100: Mix of dividend-paying stocks, growth compounders, income-generating instruments (SGBs, debt funds), arbitrage/liquid for short-term needs
- 70-89: Good mix of growth and income, some dividend holdings, adequate liquid buffer
- 50-69: Skewed toward either pure growth (no income) or pure income (no growth)
- 30-49: Almost entirely growth stocks with no income stream, or heavy dividend tilt with no growth
- 0-29: No income-generating instruments at all, or portfolio is entirely in non-yielding instruments

**What to evaluate:**
- Identify dividend-paying holdings and estimate yield
- Check for SGB interest income
- Assess liquid/arbitrage buffer for short-term needs
- Balance between growth compounders and income generators

---

## Metric 7: Skin Temperature → Market Regime Adaptability (0-100)

**Biological:** Skin temperature deviations signal inflammation or illness. Stability = health.

**Financial:** How well-positioned is the portfolio for different market regimes? Can it handle bull, bear, sideways, inflationary, deflationary environments?

**Scoring:**
- 90-100: Portfolio has components that work in each regime — equities (bull), gold (inflation/fear), debt (deflation/bear), international (domestic weakness), liquid (opportunity buffer)
- 70-89: Most regimes covered but 1-2 gaps (e.g., no inflation hedge, or no bear market defense)
- 50-69: Portfolio works in current regime but would struggle in a different one (e.g., all-equity portfolio in a sustained bull)
- 30-49: Significant regime concentration — would be badly hurt by specific scenarios (e.g., rate hikes with no floating rate exposure, or INR weakness with no international allocation)
- 0-29: Extreme regime bet — portfolio is essentially a single-regime play

**What to evaluate:**
- Check for inflation hedges (gold, commodities, real assets)
- Check for deflation/bear protection (debt, liquid, cash)
- Check for currency hedge (international allocation)
- Check for optionality (cash/liquid buffer to deploy in crashes)
- Assess sector cyclicality — all cyclicals or all defensives?

---

## Metric 8: SpO2 → Capital Deployment Efficiency (0-100)

**Biological:** Blood oxygen saturation — how effectively the body distributes oxygen. 95%+ = healthy.

**Financial:** How effectively is capital deployed? Is money working or sitting idle? Is the investment rate appropriate?

**Scoring:**
- 90-100: <5% idle capital (cash + liquid as % of total), all positions are intentionally sized, no "forgotten" holdings, clear deployment strategy for new capital
- 70-89: 5-10% idle capital, most positions intentional, some minor inefficiencies
- 50-69: 10-15% idle capital, some positions appear forgotten, no clear capital deployment strategy
- 30-49: 15-25% idle capital, multiple positions appear abandoned, no visible reinvestment
- 0-29: >25% idle capital, large cash balances earning nothing, portfolio appears dormant

**What to evaluate:**
- Calculate total idle capital (cash + liquid + arbitrage as % of total)
- Assess if idle capital is intentional dry powder or neglect
- Check for positions that appear abandoned (tiny size, deep loss, no additions)
- Estimate capital deployment rate (evidence of recent purchases)

## Presenting the Report

### Format

Present as a clean, Notion-like table or list — not terminal ASCII art. Use simple progress indicators.

```
Campfire Vitals Report
──────────────────────

Diversification (HRV)      78/100   ████████░░   Well-spread but missing international
Cost Efficiency (RHR)      58/100   ██████░░░░   Significant fragmentation
Risk Calibration (Sleep)   82/100   ████████░░   Appropriate for hold style
Growth Momentum (Strain)   91/100   █████████░   Multiple multibaggers
Rebalancing (Recovery)     48/100   █████░░░░░   Micro-positions need cleanup
Income Streams (RR)        72/100   ███████░░░   Good dividend base
Regime Adaptability (Temp) 74/100   ███████░░░   Gold hedge covers inflation
Capital Efficiency (SpO₂)  65/100   ██████░░░░   Excess liquid buffer

──────────────────────
Overall Recovery Score     71/100
──────────────────────
```

When generating as part of a React dashboard, use:
- White background, thin warm-gray dividers between rows
- Score number in bold `#37352f`
- Progress bar: thin (4px), colored by score range (green > 75, amber 50-75, red < 50), track in `#e8e8e4`
- One-line interpretation in `#787774`
- Overall score slightly larger, with a subtle top border separator

**No dark backgrounds. No terminal aesthetic. Clean, light, typographic.**

### Interpretation Paragraph

Write 3-4 sentences interpreting the scores in context. What's strong, what needs attention, what's the #1 priority fix. Use the Campfire Vitals metaphor naturally (e.g., "Your portfolio's Diversification score is strong — it can absorb sector shocks. But your Rebalancing score is low — accumulated micro-positions and fragmentation are like sleep debt that compounds silently.").

### Score Visualization

Present scores in a clean Notion-like format — white background, thin dividers, colored progress bars. When creating a React artifact, use the same Notion-like design language as the task center:
- White/warm-gray palette
- Inter font family
- Thin progress bars colored by score range
- No shadows, no dark theme, no terminal aesthetic
- Generous whitespace between metric rows
